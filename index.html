Peter Mitrano

I am a PhD student at the University of Michigan currently advised by Professr Dmitry Berenson. I started in the fall of 2018 after I completed my Bachelors in Robotics Engineering and Bachelors in Computer Science at WPI.

Research

My research interests include planning and learning for robotics, controls, and machine learning. I have read Reinforcement Learning by Sutton & Barto cover to cover, and have published about a generative music system using RNNs at a small workshop.

As a freshman at WPI, I worked as a research assistant for Russell Torris, under Dr. Sonia Chernova. I also worked with Dr. Chernova for a summer at Georgia Tech. I was treated as a graduate student, and my research focused on using demonstration to improve indoor robot navigation.

I am currently working with Professor Calo Pinciroli on a swarm robotics project. We are studing the space of possible behaviors for memoryless computeless robots, specifically showing that they are capable of n-class segregation with a single primitive sensor and no communication. You can learn more by reading the paper we wrote for the project as a part of the Swarm Intelligence course at WPI.

I am also currently working with Professor Scott Barton. Our research together is centered around machine learning and music. As a trained musician, this is an interesting application of machine learning.
